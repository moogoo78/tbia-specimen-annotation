# Closing Gaps in Specimen Metadata: A Collaborative Annotation and Feedback Platform for Natural History Collections 

While the digitization of natural history collections is accelerating globally, GBIF data trends reveal a critical bottleneck: a persistent gap in the completeness of digitized specimen records. Many records still lack essential metadata, specifically accurate taxonomic classifications, precise geospatial coordinates, and explicit collection dates. Bridging these gaps requires a systematic, scalable annotation mechanism.

Inspired by collaborative frameworks such as the European DiSSCover initiative, we developed an annotation platform tailored for digital specimens. The platform ingests natural history collection data from the Taiwan Biodiversity Information Alliance (TBIA). The platform allows users to query target specimens by data completeness or holding institution through filtering functions. To optimize the workflow, it pairs an intuitive manual interface with AI-assisted data extraction, enabling users to verify and adjust AI-generated transcriptions to increase processing efficiency.

Aggregated annotations form a trackable feedback loop, allowing enriched records to be returned to the original data providers for review and merging into their primary databases. This continuous cycle improves the quality of open data while lowering the barrier to participation for smaller institutions. Collections from institutions without sufficient staff often fail to transcribe labels fast enough to keep pace with imaging; the platform lets them publish specimen images early and recruit public contributors to fill in the data, increasing the usability of their holdings and encouraging institutions with un-digitized collections to join the biodiversity informatics community.
